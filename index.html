About

Who We Are

  • We are a group of academics and clinicians developing image processing and data analysis methods for neurological disorders.
  • We use brain images and data to understand disease mechanisms.
  • We translate image processing workflows into clinical application at University Hospital Southampton.

Rahman Attar

Lecturer of Electronics and Computer Science
Rahman Attar

Rahman Attar is a computer scientist specialising in Artificial Medical Intelligence. He currently holds the position of Lecturer (which is the British equivalent of a tenured Assistant Professor in the USA) at the Department of Electronics and Computer Science, University of Southampton. Prior to this, he worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Imperial College London. Rahman completed his PhD under the supervision of Professor Alex Frangi at the University of Sheffield and the University of Leeds. His primary research area involves combining Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Medicine to create unbiased and ethical AI technologies that can improve healthcare strategies.

Angela Darekar

UHS Imaging Research Lead

Ruihua Hou

Professor of Biological Psychiatry
Ruihua Hou

Ruihua Hou is a professor of biological psychiatry. She qualified in medicine and trained as a psychiatrist. She completed a clinical fellowship followed by her PhD in psychopharmacology, both in the Department of Psychiatry Queen’s Medical Centre at the University of Nottingham. She then joined the Department of Psychiatry at the University of Southampton. Her research interest is in human psychopharmacology focusing on the role of the locus coeruleus in the development of neurodegenerative diseases using MRI imaging technique. She led the ARUK-funded project investigating the role of the locus coeruleus using neuromelanin-sensitive MRI in Alzheimer’s disease. Her work aims to identify early biomarkers for cognitive decline and develop early intervention approaches to prevent disease progression.

Christopher Kipps

Professor of Clinical Neurology and Dementia

Sofia Michopoulou

Consultant Clinical Scientist
Sofia Michopoulou

Sofia is a Consultant Clinical Scientist and Head of Nuclear Medicine Physics at University Hospital Southampton and Honorary Senior Clinical Lecturer at University of Southampton. In her clinical role, Sofia leads a team of clinical scientists supporting and developing radionuclide therapy and diagnostic SPECT/CT and PET/CT services. She also teaches medical imaging at BSc and MSc modules and delivers part of the Fellows of Royal College of Radiology physics module. Her research focuses on the development and evaluation of imaging and inflammation biomarkers for disease characterisation and the use of artificial intelligence for dementia diagnosis support.

Ruben Sanchez-Garcia

Ruben Sanchez-Garcia

Ruben Sanchez-Garcia is a Professor in Mathematics at the University of Southampton, member of the Institute for Life Sciences, and Fellow of the Alan Turing Institute. He does interdisciplinary work in network science and applied topology motivated by real-world, particularly biomedical, applications. His research publications include work on network modelling and analysis, applied topology, and mathematical biology.

Facilitator Checklist

Scheduling

  • Set up the meeting in MS Office Calendar or equivalent scheduling tool.
  • Send meeting invites to all attendees, including venue information, meeting link (if hybrid), and agenda.
  • For hybrid meetings, confirm that the booked room has well-functioning video and sound equipment for virtual attendees.
  • (One week before) Send reminders to all attendees.

On the day

  • Send reminders to all attendees with login/location information.
  • Practice sharing slides or screen.
  • Adjust room lighting as needed.
  • If audio is used, test computer connection to speakers and adjust sound system.
In-person Presentations
  • Test computer connection to screen/projector.
  • Test presentation on screen/projector.
Virtual Presentations
  • Login 15-30 minutes early to test with co-hosts (if applicable).
  • Make sure to have a strong internet connection; use LAN if possible.
  • Test webcam prior to the meeting.
  • Shut down unnecessary computer programs.
The meeting link will be shared closer to the scheduled date. Stay tuned!
